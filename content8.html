Summary:Before We Go

So far, we discussed how we can disseminate Narco narratives using Quijano’s coloniality of power and Hall’s concept of discursive othering to show the underlying colonial legacies structure in both economic exploitation and cultural representation of Hispanic communities. These frameworks explain why Cartel narratives contribute to the expulsion and ignorance of Latino laborers in America. Simultaneously, American media’s fixation on cartel narratives from dramas like Narcos to news coverage commodifies fear and implies Hispanic people as perpetual threats, and legitimizes punitive policies that are happening as we speak. This feedback loop perpetuates socioeconomic precarity and racial hierarchies. Dismantling these oppressive systems requires a long process, but most importantly, the understanding of the situation from all of us. So after reading through this post, what steps will you take to challenge these entrenched systems and create pathways for justice and dignity for all?
