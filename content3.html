Case Study: Netflix's Narcos Series

As the most popular Drug and Crime related shows in recents years, the Narcos and Narcos: Mexico are Netflix mega projects that each stretches three seasons and totals 30 episodes. Since 2015, the Narcos duology has become one of the highest rated and most viewed crime shows on Netflix.

Although this series is widely considered as a fairly unbiased production of cartel stories, it still faces criticism. Despite the main actors, script writers, and directors who are also Hispanic claiming that the stories are not the ussual "Good" Westerners vs. "Bad" Latinos plot, it is still critisized among Latin American communities as nothing but selling stereotypes the West want to see (Mckenna, n.d.). In fact, many Colombian viewers strongly resent this show, as they never want Escobar be the icon let alone the only icon of this country. And they want to make sure people do not think average Colombians treat him as a hero (Brown, 2015). However, Narcos series centers around the rise and fall as Escobar, and dramalized and emphasized his family relations, criminal mastermind, generosity, and overlooked many atrocities he commited, which are forever wounds to Colombian people.

For audiences with no prior knowledge, it often appears as a quasi-history despite containing many dramatic elements and lacking converage for many crucial events, and they will believe that Colombia and Mexico are only about corruption and violence as in the show (Lara, 2025).
